Enjoy a [springtime Parks On The Air activation][party] while participating in the
2026 North Dakota QSO Party.
<!--more-->

[party]: {{< relref "/calendar/2026/pota-activation-for-the-nd-qso-party.md" >}}

{{< figure src="https://www.history.nd.gov/historicsites/abercrombie/images/abercrombiesplash.jpg" link="https://www.history.nd.gov/historicsites/abercrombie" >}}

RRRA will be operating a club station for the [ND QSO Party] from [Fort
Abercrombie State Historic Site][fort] in Abercrombie, ND. This will also
double as a [Parks On The Air activation][pota].

[ND QSO Party]: {{< relref "/calendar/2026/nd-qso-party.md" >}}
[fort]: https://www.history.nd.gov/historicsites/abercrombie
[pota]: https://pota.app/#/activations

All hams are welcome to stop by, operate, and see what Parks On The Air
is all about. Participants will be able to operate the club callsign
(W0ILO) or operate on their own from the site to count as their own POTA
activation.

Multi-operator stations are not eligible to win awards in the QSO Party,
so the primary objective is to get on the air and **have fun**.

Setup will begin at 10:30am, the QSO Party starts at 1pm. Radios and
antennas will be provided, but hams are welcome to bring their own equipment
to try out if they'd like.

Please note there will be no public restrooms available, and services in
Abercrombie are limited (ie: plan accordingly; if you want something, bring it).

Talk-in will be on 146.520 MHz (the location is just outside reliable
repeater range).

{{< banner >}}
In the event of inclement weather, this event will be cancelled. Notice
of cancellation will be sent to the RRRA Club Announcements and Club
Discussion  mailing lists.

Please direct all questions about RRRA mailing lists to the [Webmaster
Team](mailto:webmaster@rrra.org).
