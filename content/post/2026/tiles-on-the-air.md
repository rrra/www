>Tiles on the Air shares DNA with programs like POTA and SOTA, and
>layers on top by adding physical activity and a unique location system.
>Think of it as SOTA, POTA, and an exercise tracking app having a child.
>Your existing POTA and SOTA activations can count for Tiles too.
>
>Activate Anywhere
>: Tiles on the Air lets you activate from any Maidenhead grid tile,
>as long as it's safe for you and others and you are allowed to operate
>there. POTA and SOTA cover designated parks and summits; Tiles fills in
>the rest: a backyard, a local trail, a beach, a ridgeline.
>
>Fitness Meets Radio
>: Physical activity distance increases your score. The more you walk,
>hike, bike, ruck, or paddle, the more points you add. Mobile (/M)
>activations score without distance on the same leaderboard. Designed to
>get ham operators on the air, however they reach the tile.
>
>Stack Your Activities
>: Tiles on the Air is designed to complement other programs, not
>replace them. A summit activation can count for both Tiles and SOTA. A
>park activation counts for both Tiles and POTA. Stack your activations
>to maximize your time in the field.
>
>Easy to get started
>: A Tiles activation is valid with just 1 QSO. So even on days when the bands
>are rough or your equipment is modest, you still come home with something
>logged. Work more QSOs to score more points; your 1st QSO already puts you on
>the board. [^2]

### What is a Tile?

>A Tile is a Maidenhead subsquare, roughly 5 × 7 km at mid-latitudes
>(about the size of a neighborhood). Every inch of the planet is inside
>some tile: a backyard, a park, a trailhead, a beach, all tiles. Activate
>from anywhere as long as it's safe for you and others and you are
>allowed to operate there. [^2]
